🌍 Journey to Kolkata 🌍

Day 1: Belur Math

📷 Welcome to Kolkata! Our first destination is Belur Math, a famous religious institution and pilgrimage center. 🙏

Belur Math is located on the western bank of the Hooghly River and is known for its serene atmosphere. The main temple is dedicated to Sri Ramakrishna, the spiritual guru of Swami Vivekananda. The architecture of the temple is a blend of Hindu, Christian, and Islamic styles. 🕌

Don't forget to capture the beautiful architecture and peaceful surroundings with your camera. 📸

Day 1: Eco Park

🌳 After visiting Belur Math, let's head to Eco Park, one of the largest parks in India. 🌿

Eco Park is spread over an area of 480 acres and offers a wide range of recreational activities. You can explore the different zones, including a waterbody with boating facilities, a butterfly garden, a bamboo garden, and a rose garden. 🦋🌹

As a photography enthusiast, you'll find plenty of opportunities to capture the beauty of nature and the eco-friendly attractions like the solar-powered toy train and the musical fountain. 🎶📷

Day 2: Victoria Memorial

🏰 Today, we'll visit the iconic Victoria Memorial, a grand marble building dedicated to Queen Victoria. 🎩

The museum inside the memorial houses a collection of paintings, sculptures, and artifacts from the colonial era. The surrounding gardens are perfect for a leisurely stroll and provide a picturesque backdrop for your photographs. 🌸📸

Make sure to immerse yourself in the rich history and cultural heritage showcased in this architectural masterpiece. 🏛️

Day 3: Howrah Bridge

🌉 On our last day, we'll visit the iconic Howrah Bridge, one of the busiest cantilever bridges in the world. 🌉

The steel bridge offers a stunning view of the Hooghly River and the city skyline. It's a perfect spot for capturing breathtaking photographs of the river and the bustling city below. 📷

Remember, pedestrians are not allowed on the bridge, so make sure to get the best shots from a safe and designated viewing area. 🚶‍♀️🚶‍♂️
